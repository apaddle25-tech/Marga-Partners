The Marga Commercialization Framework

One continuous path from science to sustained adoption.

A
Aneesh Patel, PharmDFounder, Marga Partners

The Marga Commercialization Framework begins from a single, unfashionable premise: commercialization is not a phase that starts at launch. It is a thread that runs through the entire life of a product, from the first decisions in trial design to the mature management of a franchise years later. Treat it as a late stage and you inherit constraints. Treat it as a through-line and you get to shape them.

The framework maps the decisions that carry a product from molecule to market as one connected sequence rather than a series of disconnected departmental handoffs. Its job is to make the scientific, medical, regulatory, and commercial choices reinforce one another instead of surprising one another, so that by the time a product launches, nothing about the market has to be discovered late.

Commercialization is not the last stage of development. It is the through-line of the whole thing.

From handoffs to a through-line

In most organizations, a product is passed down a line: research hands to development, development to regulatory, regulatory to commercial, each group optimizing its own stage and tossing the result over the wall. Value leaks at every wall. The framework replaces that relay with a shared spine: a small number of checkpoints where the whole path is considered at once, so an early scientific choice is already made with the eventual commercial story in view.

Concretely, that means asking commercial questions while the science is still soft enough to answer them. What claim will the market need, and does the trial produce it? What will the story be, and does the evidence support it? Where might this product go next, and what would we have to establish now to be allowed there later? None of these are launch questions. They are development questions wearing commercial clothes, and the framework insists they be asked while the answers are still cheap.

What the framework produces

The output is not a document that sits on a shelf. It is a plan in which every stage already speaks the language of the next: an endpoint chosen with the claim in mind, a label shaped with the message in mind, a launch built on a story that has been true since Phase II. Because the decisions were connected from the start, the launch becomes the delivery of a strategy rather than the frantic assembly of one.

Used early, the framework turns the seams between functions, the very places where value usually leaks, into the places where advantage is deliberately built. It is most powerful before a program is locked, when the choices that will define the commercial future are still open. Applied late, it can still diagnose and repair. Applied early, it prevents the expensive translations that late-stage commercialization spends its life performing.

Design the path as one continuous argument, and launch becomes delivery instead of rescue.

The Marga Commercialization Framework is, in the end, a discipline for treating development and commercialization as the single continuous path they always were. It refuses the artificial line between making a product and making it succeed, and it puts the commercial mind in the room early enough to matter, which is the only place it has ever truly mattered.
